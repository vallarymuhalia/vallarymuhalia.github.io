About Me

I am a Data Scientist at Meta working on turning messy behavioral and security problems into measurable, high-impact decisions into measurable, high-impact decisions. I work on problems where user access patterns, risk signals, and operational complexity demand constant measurement and iteration to guide product direction.

Most of my career has been about extracting clarity from ambiguity in high-stakes environments. At Meta, that's meant building AI-driven auto-decisioning systems that reduce friction while maintaining security—understanding how to optimize precision and recall when both user experience and risk matter equally. In Ads Monetization, I generated $800M+ in incremental revenue by developing scalable experimentation frameworks that balanced monetization with customer experience, using NLP and ML models to unlock insights from complex behavioral data. Throughout, I've been most energized when the problem requires balancing competing priorities—like protecting users while enabling seamless access, or growing revenue while respecting customer preferences

I studied Computer Science and Statistics at the University of Chicago, where I developed a foundation in both rigorous analysis and systems thinking—a combination that shapes how I approach problems where data, behavior, and product decisions intersect.

Outside of work, I'm an avid runner—I've completed 4 marathons in the past 2 years alongside numerous half marathons and 5Ks. I'm also a travel enthusiast with a dream to visit as many countries as I can, driven by curiosity about how people and cultures work across the globe.
